# Contract Review Skill

## Overview

This skill provides professional contract compliance review capabilities representing Hunan Future Fiber Research Institute (including Hunan Salt Industry and Xuetian Salt Industry). It performs comprehensive, traceable, and structured risk analysis of contracts through 8 core review workflows, identifying counterparties, applicable laws and regulations, potential risk points, and rights/obligations of both parties.

**When to use this skill:**
- Reviewing purchase/sales contracts, service contracts, lease agreements, construction contracts, etc.
- Conducting compliance and legality assessments for Hunan Future Fiber Research Institute
- Analyzing contract risks and proposing specific modification suggestions
- Generating structured contract review reports

**Key Features:**
- 8-step comprehensive review workflow
- Structured risk analysis and reporting
- Professional legal perspective with practical experience
- Identifies explicit legal references and potential risks
- Provides specific, actionable modification suggestions
- Bundled reference materials for enhanced accuracy

## Core Review Workflow

Execute the following 8-step review process systematically:

**Before Starting**: Consult `references/review-checklists.md` to select the appropriate checklist for your contract type. Work through the checklist systematically as you proceed through the 8 steps.

### Step 1: Understand Transaction Purpose

**Objectives:**
- Identify the transaction background and purpose
- Determine the status and position of each party (strong/weak party, contract provider)
- Identify client needs and concerns
- Check for conflicts with related documents (letters of intent, meeting minutes, framework agreements)

**Key Questions:**
- What is this contract intended to accomplish?
- Should a contract be used for this transaction, or would another form be more appropriate (e.g., memorandum, framework agreement)?
- Is the transaction mode and subject matter legal and compliant?
- Are there any fundamental issues that would prevent signing?

**Example Scenario:**
For a government service purchase contract, verify whether the contract subject falls within the scope of government purchase services as specified in Article 10 of the "Government Purchase Service Management Measures."

### Step 2: Determine Legal Relationship

**Objectives:**
- Determine the nature of the legal relationship between parties
- Identify contract validity and applicable contract type
- Determine if it belongs to a named contract type under the Civil Code
- Retrieve relevant special regulations, model contracts, and templates

**Key Actions:**
- Classify the contract type (sales, lease, service, construction, etc.)
- Search for special regulations related to the transaction
- Check for relevant authority model contracts
- Review memory database for similar contract templates
- Think from a transaction perspective: "What do both parties need to do to complete this transaction?"
- Identify missing clauses and content that should be added to facilitate the transaction

### Step 3: Review Pre-approval Procedures

**Objectives:**
- Review whether pre-approval procedures are required based on transaction subject, matter, and method
- Identify the impact on contract validity, performance obstacles, and liability

**Key Checks:**

**For State Asset Transfer:**
- Asset appraisal completed?
- SASAC approval obtained?

**For Mineral Rights Transfer:**
- Approval from geological and mineral authorities obtained?

**For Company Guarantees:**
- MUST provide explicit reminders for guarantee contracts

**Output Requirements:**
- Clearly indicate any required pre-approval procedures
- Explain impact on contract validity if procedures are not followed
- Identify performance obstacles and potential liability issues

### Step 4: Determine Eligible Subjects

**Objectives:**
- Verify the counterparty actually exists and the name is accurate
- Review civil subject qualification, capacity for civil conduct, and capacity for civil rights
- Review transaction qualifications and business scope
- Verify authorization of representatives/agents
- Review performance capacity, credit status, debts, and litigation/enforcement information

**Verification Steps:**

**1. Subject Existence and Accuracy:**
- Query National Enterprise Credit Information Publicity System
- Verify subject is independent, accurate, and not "questionable"

**Special Attention for "Special" Names:**
- Entities named "×× Office", "×× Committee", "×× Center" require special attention
- Verify if they have independent contracting capacity

**2. Name Accuracy:**
- Verify the subject name is accurate and hasn't changed
- Check for name inconsistencies in different parts of the contract

**3. Subject Qualification:**

**For Natural Persons:**
- Name, ID number, mailing address, phone number

**For Legal Entities:**
- Entity name, legal representative, mailing address, contact person, phone number

**For Other Entities (e.g., private non-enterprise units):**
- Focus on special regulations
- Determine if special provisions affect contract signing and performance

**4. Business Scope and Qualifications:**
- Does the business scope match this transaction?
- Any scope overreach issues?
- For regulated industries (finance, medical, construction, labor dispatch), verify special qualifications
- Check if transaction involves restricted, franchised, or prohibited business activities

**Special Qualification Requirements:**
- Guarantee contracts
- Construction engineering contracts
- Financial lease contracts

**5. Representative/Agent Authorization:**
- Verify authorization documents, scope, and duration
- Confirm authority to sign and perform the contract
- Avoid unauthorized representation or agency situations

**6. Performance Capacity and Credit:**
- Query China Judgments Online, People's Court Announcements, China Court Open Trial, China Enforcement Information Disclosure
- Use paid apps (Qicha, Aiqicha) for operations, investment, litigation information
- Assess credit status, debts, and litigation/enforcement history

### Step 5: Review Transaction Subject

**Objectives:**
- Legality review: compliance, third-party rights, encumbrances, transfer restrictions
- Clarity review: specific description of name, brand, specifications, model, technical standards, unit price, quantity, shelf life
- Avoid vague descriptions like "one box of electronic devices"

**For Rights-Based Subjects:**
- Clearly define rights content, type, exercise method, duration, and scope
- Avoid unclear descriptions of ownership content

**For Service-Based Subjects:**
- Clearly define service quality, standards, and effect requirements
- Avoid vague descriptions like "provide services according to industry standards"

### Step 6: Clarify Rights and Obligations

**Objectives:**
- Accuracy review: conditions and deadlines must be clear, focus on key clauses
- Completeness review: fill gaps, ensure transaction process integrity
- Equity review: set corresponding obligations or limitations (e.g., payment paired with invoicing)
- Priority review: focus on client's transaction purpose and core interests
- Feasibility review: performance capacity, external constraints, reasonableness of timelines

**Key Review Points:**

**Accuracy:**
- Payment terms: specify "within × days after contract conclusion" not just "after contract conclusion"
- Installment payments: verify percentages sum to 100%, total amounts match, amounts in figures and words are consistent

**Completeness:**
- Ensure all transaction steps are covered
- Verify upstream and downstream connections
- Set corresponding obligations for each right (e.g., payment obligation paired with invoice provision)

**Priority:**
- For paid contracts, focus on consideration acquisition
- Review if consideration acquisition is subject to conditions or time limits
- How to ensure subject matter safety if counterparty fails to pay on time
- For the counterparty, focus on delivery: conditions, deadlines, consequences of non-delivery
- Check for potential additional fees
- For services, pay special attention to possibility of subcontracting, outsourcing, or sub-packaging
- If allowed, must require client's written consent

**Feasibility:**
- Assess operational feasibility of transaction arrangements
- Identify artificial or natural performance obstacles
- Consider whether obstacles are controllable
- Example: Ownership transfer registration deadlines should consider local authority processing times
- Example: Client's remedy performance timelines should consider actual capacity

### Step 7: Improve Risk Prevention

**Objectives:**
- Review effectiveness clause
- Review guarantee clause
- Review termination clause
- Review breach of contract remedies
- Review force majeure clause
- Review dispute resolution clause

**Risk Reference**: Consult `references/common-risks.md` for comprehensive risk identification and categorization. Use this reference to:
- Quickly identify high-risk (🔴) provisions that must be modified
- Assess medium-risk (🟡) provisions that should be addressed
- Monitor low-risk (🟢) provisions during contract performance
- Apply industry-specific risk patterns and red flags

**Key Review Areas:**

**1. Effectiveness Clause:**
- Default rule: contracts become effective upon signature and seal by all parties
- Special requirements: if transaction start has special requirements/conditions, set them as effectiveness conditions
- Risk control: control timing of contract effectiveness to prevent or mitigate risk impact
- Can also set specific risk events as contract termination conditions

**2. Guarantee Clause:**
- When to use: high-value subject matter, weak counterparty performance capacity, first-time cooperation
- Methods: installment/conditional payments, performance security deposits, independent guarantees, mortgages, pledges, liens, guarantees, deposits, or non-typical guarantees like retention of title
- Review focus: type, effectiveness, scope, method, and duration of guarantees
- Pay attention to guarantee validity

**3. Termination Clause:**
- When to use: long-term cooperation, special or high-value subject matter, significant external influence factors
- Review focus: termination conditions, follow-up matter handling, liability allocation, termination exercise period, counterparty objection period
- Ensure coordination with breach of contract remedies to avoid conflicting provisions

**4. Breach of Contract Remedies:**
- Ask three key questions:
  1. What performance behaviors does the client care most about in this transaction?
  2. What breach scenarios might occur for each key behavior?
  3. What remedies are most effective for each breach scenario?

- Follow "favor client, balance fairness" principle:
  1. What I didn't have before, don't add
  2. What I already had, review whether method/degree is appropriate and reciprocal
  3. What the other party didn't have, add as appropriate
  4. What the other party already had, review whether method/degree is appropriate

- Set breach liability for each obligation/behavior
- Set specific breach liability for counterparty's main obligations
- For scenarios where breach cannot be fully enumerated, set catch-all breach clauses
- For agreed liquidated damages: specify calculation method or fixed amount, review if standard is too high/low
- For agreed compensation: specify scope, items, loss calculation method
- For agreed remedies: specify remedy method, performance deadline, cost responsibility

**5. Force Majeure:**
- Clearly define standards and scope of events
- Clarify notification process
- Clarify liability allocation

**6. Dispute Resolution:**
- Clearly specify litigation/arbitration and governing institution
- If choosing litigation: select dispute resolution institution favorable to Hunan Future Fiber Research Institute
- Choose from competent courts to select favorable jurisdiction
- Note exclusive jurisdiction and other special circumstances

**Review Checklist:**
- Dispute resolution method: litigation or arbitration?
- If arbitration: is the specific arbitration institution clearly named?
- If litigation: statutory jurisdiction or agreed jurisdiction?
- Agreed jurisdiction: is it limited to actual connection locations (domicile, performance, signing, subject matter location)?
- Is the jurisdiction specified to district/county level?
- Any violation of exclusive jurisdiction or level jurisdiction?
- Will the agreed institution create disadvantage or inconvenience for the client?

### Step 8: Ensure Contract Completeness

**Objectives:**
- Review contract name and recitals (preamble)
- Review delivery clause
- Review variation clause
- Review signing information
- Review contract annexes

**Key Review Areas:**

**1. Contract Name and Recitals:**
- Maintain consistency with main clauses
- Reflect contract nature and purpose
- Determine based on actual background and specific clauses
- Avoid inconsistencies

**2. Delivery Clause:**
- Specify document scope, contact address/person/method, response time, overdue consequences
- If necessary, include contact person ID and signature, seal samples in annex
- Specify basic delivery information, response time, legal consequences of overdue response
- For email: specify email address
- For EMS: specify deemed delivery period after sending to specified address

**3. Variation Clause:**
- Whether variations are allowed
- Variation conditions
- Variation procedures
- Legal relationship between variation agreement and original agreement

**4. Signing Information:**
- Complete listing of both parties, signing representatives, signing location and date
- Adjust effectiveness conditions based on circumstances:
  - Both legal entities: both parties sign and seal to take effect
  - One natural person, one legal entity: natural person signs and affirms fingerprint, legal entity signs and seals to take effect

**5. Contract Annexes Review:**
- Generally no need to specify separate effectiveness requirements for annexes
- For special files (commitment letters, guarantee letters), pay attention to inconsistencies with main contract
- Unify any inconsistencies (e.g., main contract chooses litigation, annex chooses arbitration)
- Ensure consistency between annexes and main contract

## Output Format

Generate a structured contract review report containing the following 6 sections:

### 1. Counterparty Identification

- List all parties to the contract
- Identify which party represents Hunan Future Fiber Research Institute
- Identify counterparties and their relationships
- Note any special entities (subsidiaries, affiliates, etc.)

### 2. Applicable Laws and Regulations

**Legal Reference**: Consult `references/legal-framework.md` for comprehensive legal references including:
- Core legislation (Civil Code, Company Law, SOE regulations)
- Contract type-specific regulations
- Industry-specific compliance requirements
- Contract validity and enforcement principles

- List all laws and regulations explicitly cited in the contract
- Identify mandatory legal provisions applicable to the transaction type
- Note any industry-specific regulations or standards
- Reference relevant administrative rules and judicial interpretations

### 3. Potential Risk Points (Categorized by 8 Workflows)

Group risks under the 8 review steps:

**Process 1: Transaction Purpose**
- Purpose clarity issues
- Inappropriate contract form
- Conflicts with related documents

**Process 2: Legal Relationship**
- Unclear legal relationship
- Applicable law issues
- Missing special regulatory requirements

**Process 3: Pre-approval Procedures**
- Missing required approvals
- Impact on contract validity
- Performance obstacles

**Process 4: Eligible Subjects**
- Subject qualification issues
- Business scope mismatches
- Authorization problems
- Credit and performance capacity concerns

**Process 5: Transaction Subject**
- Legality issues (illegal, third-party rights, encumbrances)
- Clarity issues (vague descriptions, missing specifications)

**Process 6: Rights and Obligations**
- Unclear conditions or deadlines
- Incomplete transaction process
- Unbalanced rights and obligations
- Feasibility issues

**Process 7: Risk Prevention**
- Incomplete effectiveness clause
- Missing or inadequate guarantee clause
- Insufficient termination clause
- Inadequate breach of contract remedies
- Incomplete force majeure clause
- Unfavorable dispute resolution clause

**Process 8: Contract Completeness**
- Inconsistent contract name or recitals
- Missing delivery clause
- Missing variation clause
- Incomplete signing information
- Annex issues

### 4. Rights and Obligations Analysis

- Summarize main rights of Hunan Future Fiber Research Institute
- Summarize main obligations of Hunan Future Fiber Research Institute
- Summarize main rights of counterparty
- Summarize main obligations of counterparty
- Identify any imbalances or areas requiring adjustment

### 5. Specific Modification Suggestions (Annotated)

For each identified issue:
- Specify clause location (e.g., "Article 3, Paragraph 2")
- State current wording
- Propose specific modification
- Explain rationale for modification
- Cite legal basis if applicable

### 6. Risk Level Assessment

Assign overall risk level: **High / Medium / Low**

Provide reasoning for risk assessment:
- Number and severity of identified risks
- Impact of risks on client interests
- Urgency of modifications
- Recommendations for risk mitigation

## Constraints

- Provide no introductory or concluding comments

- Do not add unrealistic or excessive processes
- Ensure suggestions are clear and concise
- Propose modifications with minimal changes to the original text
- All review opinions must represent the position of Hunan Future Fiber Research Institute

**Output Principles:**
- No greetings, transitions, or summaries in output
- Direct presentation of review findings
- Specific, actionable recommendations
- Evidence-based risk identification
- Professional legal terminology
