# Common Contract Risks and Red Flags

**Last Updated:** 2026-04-14
**Purpose**: Quick reference for identifying high-risk contract provisions

---

## Risk Categorization

**High Risk (🔴)**: Must be modified or contract should not be signed
**Medium Risk (🟡)**: Should be modified if possible
**Low Risk (🟢)**: Minor issues, monitor during performance

---

## 🔴 High Risk Issues (Must Fix)

### 1. Unilateral Termination Rights
**Pattern**: One party can terminate without cause while the other cannot
**Example**: "Party A may terminate this agreement at any time without liability"
**Risk**: Severe imbalance, exposes client to sudden contract termination
**Action**: Require reciprocal termination rights or reasonable notice periods

### 2. Excessive Liquidated Damages
**Pattern**: Penalty amounts grossly disproportionate to actual damages
**Example**: "In case of breach, Party B shall pay 10x the contract value as liquidated damages"
**Risk**: May be unenforceable, creates unreasonable liability
**Action**: Cap at reasonable estimate of actual damages (typically 20-30% of contract value)

### 3. Unlimited Liability Clauses
**Pattern**: No limitation on liability for breaches or indemnities
**Example**: "Party A shall be liable for all losses, damages, and expenses without limitation"
**Risk**: Exposes client to uncapped liability, potentially catastrophic
**Action**: Insert liability caps (e.g., "limited to the contract value" or "limited to insurance coverage")

### 4. Unfair Indemnification Provisions
**Pattern**: Broad indemnity for losses beyond indemnifying party's control
**Example**: "Party B shall indemnify Party A against all claims, regardless of fault"
**Risk**: Unfair allocation of risk, may be unenforceable
**Action**: Limit indemnification to losses caused by indemnifying party's negligence or breach

### 5. Automatic Renewal Clauses
**Pattern**: Contract automatically renews without explicit consent
**Example**: "This contract shall automatically renew for successive one-year terms unless cancelled 30 days before expiration"
**Risk**: Client may be bound to unfavorable terms for extended periods
**Action**: Require explicit written consent for renewal or extend cancellation notice period

### 6. Unreasonable Delay Penalties
**Pattern**: Excessive daily penalties for minor delays
**Example**: "Delay penalty shall be 5% of contract value per day"
**Risk:": Penalties accumulate quickly, may exceed actual damages
**Action**: Cap daily penalties and link them to actual damages

### 7. One-Sided Force Majeure
**Pattern**: Only one party can claim force majeure exemption
**Example**: "Party A shall be excused from performance for force majeure events"
**Risk**: Unfair allocation of uncontrollable risks
**Action**: Ensure force majeure provisions apply equally to both parties

### 8. Unclear Dispute Resolution
**Pattern**: Dispute resolution mechanism favors counterparty
**Example**: "Disputes shall be resolved in courts located in counterparty's remote jurisdiction"
**Risk**: Inconvenient litigation, increased legal costs
**Action**: Specify neutral jurisdiction or arbitration convenient to client

## 🟡 Medium Risk Issues (Should Fix)

### 1. Vague Payment Terms
**Pattern**: Payment timing or amount unclear
**Example**: "Payment shall be made within a reasonable time after delivery"
**Risk**: Disputes over when payment is due
**Action**: Specify exact timing (e.g., "within 30 days after delivery")

### 2. Imbalanced Rights and Obligations
**Pattern**: One party has significantly more rights than obligations
**Example**: Extensive warranties required from client but minimal guarantees from counterparty
**Risk**: Unfair bargaining position
**Action**: Balance rights and obligations or adjust consideration

### 3. Inadequate Delivery Clauses
**Pattern**: Delivery specifications unclear or missing
**Example**: "Delivery shall be made according to industry standards"
**Risk**: Disputes over delivery quality and timing
**Action**: Specify detailed delivery requirements and acceptance criteria

### 4. Missing Change Order Procedures
**Pattern**: No process for handling scope changes
**Example**: No provision for modifications to work scope
**Risk**: Scope creep, cost overruns, disputes
**Action**: Include clear change order procedures with cost and timeline adjustments

### 5. Insufficient Insurance Requirements
**Pattern**: Insurance requirements don't adequately cover risks
**Example**: "Party shall maintain insurance as required by law"
**Risk**: Inadequate protection against foreseeable risks
**Action**: Specify insurance types, coverage amounts, and additional insured requirements

### 6. Unclear Acceptance Criteria
**Pattern**: Acceptance standards vague or subjective
**Example**: "Work shall be performed in a workmanlike manner"
**Risk**: Disputes over whether work meets standards
**Action**: Define objective, measurable acceptance criteria

### 7. Weak Confidentiality Provisions
**Pattern**: Confidentiality obligations too narrow or short
**Example**: "Confidential information shall be protected for 1 year"
**Risk**: Inadequate protection of sensitive information
**Action**: Extend confidentiality period and broaden definition of confidential information

### 8. Inadequate Representations and Warranties
**Pattern**: Limited warranties from counterparty
**Example**: No warranties about counterparty's authority or capacity
**Risk**: Client may have limited recourse if counterparty breaches
**Action**: Add appropriate representations and warranties

## 🟢 Low Risk Issues (Monitor)

### 1. Minor Formatting Inconsistencies
**Pattern**: Inconsistent terminology or formatting
**Example**: "Party A" in some sections, "Company" in others
**Risk**: Potential confusion, but rarely causes legal issues
**Action**: Standardize terminology for clarity

### 2. Non-Material Missing Clauses
**Pattern**: Minor clauses not included
**Example**: Missing "severability" clause
**Risk**: Minimal impact, most jurisdictions have default rules
**Action**: Add if client wants comprehensive protection, but not critical

### 3. Suboptimal but Workable Terms
**Pattern**: Terms not ideal but acceptable
**Example**: 60-day notice for termination when 30 days is preferred
**Risk**: Minor inconvenience, but not deal-breaking
**Action**: Negotiate improvement if possible, otherwise acceptable

## Industry-Specific Red Flags

### Construction Contracts
- **Cost-plus pricing without guaranteed maximum price**
- **Unclear change order procedures**
- **Inadequate delay damage provisions**
- **Missing liquidated damages for delay**
- **Unclear dispute resolution for technical issues**

### Technology Contracts
- **Unclear IP ownership of deliverables**
- **Insufficient confidentiality protections**
- **Weak acceptance criteria for technical deliverables**
- **Inadequate support and maintenance provisions**
- **Missing data protection and privacy provisions**

### Lease Agreements
- **Unreasonable rent escalation clauses**
- **Unclear maintenance responsibility allocation**
- **Restrictions on use that are too narrow**
- **Inadequate default remedies**
- **Missing renewal options**

### Service Contracts
- **Unclear service level definitions**
- **Inadequate performance measurement mechanisms**
- **Weak termination for convenience rights**
- **Unclear intellectual property ownership**
- **Missing transition assistance provisions**

## Risk Assessment Framework

### Step 1: Identify Risk Level
Use the categorization above (🔴 High / 🟡 Medium / 🟢 Low)

### Step 2: Assess Impact
- **Financial Impact**: Potential monetary loss
- **Operational Impact**: Disruption to business operations
- **Reputational Impact**: Harm to client's reputation
- **Legal Impact**: Exposure to litigation or regulatory action

### Step 3: Determine Action
- **Must Fix**: Modify or reject contract
- **Should Fix**: Negotiate modification, but may proceed if unsuccessful
- **Monitor**: Accept as-is but monitor during performance

### Step 4: Document Rationale
Record risk assessment and recommended actions in review report

## Common Risk Patterns by Contract Type

### Purchase/Sales Contracts
- Highest Risk: Payment terms, delivery specifications, warranty provisions
- Most Common Disputes: Quality disputes, delivery delays, payment timing

### Service Contracts
- Highest Risk: Scope definition, performance standards, termination rights
- Most Common Disputes: Service quality, scope creep, payment withholdings

### Lease Agreements
- Highest Risk: Rent amount, maintenance responsibilities, default remedies
- Most Common Disputes: Rent increases, maintenance issues, early termination

### Construction Contracts
- Highest Risk: Scope definition, change orders, delay damages
- Most Common Disputes: Scope changes, delay claims, quality defects

### Technology Contracts
- Highest Risk: IP ownership, acceptance criteria, confidentiality
- Most Common Disputes: IP ownership, technical specifications, payment milestones

## Risk Mitigation Strategies

### Prevention
- Use comprehensive checklists
- Conduct thorough due diligence
- Include clear dispute resolution mechanisms
- Specify detailed acceptance criteria

### Allocation
- Allocate risks to parties best able to manage them
- Use insurance to transfer risk
- Include indemnification provisions
- Set liability caps

### Monitoring
- Establish performance reporting requirements
- Include regular review meetings
- Specify audit rights
- Set up early warning systems

### Remedies
- Include specific breach remedies
- Set appropriate liquidated damages
- Provide for specific performance
- Include termination rights

---

*Use this reference to quickly identify and assess common contract risks. Always tailor risk assessment to the specific client and transaction context.*
