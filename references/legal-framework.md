## Regulatory Compliance Requirements

### State-Owned Enterprise (SOE) Transactions

#### Asset Transfer Approvals
- **Asset Appraisal Requirement**: State-owned assets must be appraised before transfer
- **SASAC Approval**: Required for transfers exceeding threshold amounts
- **Public Listing**: Some transfers must be publicly listed

#### Financing Guarantees
- **Corporate Law Article 15**: Company guarantees require board/shareholder approval
- **SASAC Regulations**: SOE external guarantees require special approval
- **Guarantee Limits**: Total guarantees cannot exceed 50% of net assets

#### Related Party Transactions
- **Disclosure Requirements**: Related parties must be disclosed
- **Approval Requirements**: Related party transactions require independent director approval
- **Pricing Requirements**: Must follow arm's length pricing principles

### Government Procurement

#### Government Procurement Law (2014 Amendment)
- **Procurement Methods**: Public tendering, invitation to tender, competitive negotiation, etc.
- **Procurement Thresholds**: Varies by procurement type and level
- **Procurement Procedures**: Strict requirements for bid evaluation and contract awarding

#### Government Service Procurement Management Measures
- **Service Scope**: Limited to specific government services
- **Procurement Methods**: Special rules for service procurement
- **Performance Management**: Requirements for service performance evaluation

## Contract Validity and Enforcement

### Contract Validity

#### Grounds for Contract Invalidity (Civil Code Arts. 153-154)
- Fraudulent conclusion with malicious collusion
- Malicious collusion to harm legitimate interests of third parties
- Concealing illegal intent in a nominally valid contract
- Violation of mandatory provisions of laws and administrative regulations

#### Grounds for Contract Voidability (Civil Code Arts. 147-151)
- Fraud
- Coercion
- Duress
- Material mistake
- Unconscionability

### Contract Performance

#### Force Majeure (Civil Code Arts. 180, 590)
- **Definition**: Unforeseeable, unavoidable, insurmountable objective circumstances
- **Effect**: Partial or complete exemption from liability
- **Notice Requirement**: Affected party must promptly notify the other party
- **Proof Requirement**: Affected party must provide proof

#### Change of Circumstances (Civil Code Art. 533)
- **Definition**: Fundamental change in circumstances on which contract was based
- **Requirements**: Not foreseen, not commercial risk, not force majeure
- **Effect**: Parties may renegotiate; court may modify or terminate contract

### Breach of Contract

#### Types of Breach (Civil Code Arts. 577-584)
- **Non-performance**: Failure to perform obligations
- **Late performance**: Performance after due date
- **Defective performance**: Non-conforming performance
- **Anticipatory breach**: Indication that breach will occur

#### Remedies (Civil Code Arts. 577-585)
- **Specific Performance**: Court orders performance
- **Remedial Measures**: Cure, replacement, return of payment
- **Damages**: Compensation for losses
- **Liquidated Damages**: Pre-agreed damages for breach
- **Deposit**: Forfeiture or double return

## Dispute Resolution

### Litigation

#### Civil Procedure Law (2021 Amendment)
- **Jurisdiction**: General jurisdiction (domicile), special jurisdiction (contract performance place)
- **Jurisdiction Clauses**: Parties may agree on jurisdiction in writing
- **Exclusive Jurisdiction**: Real estate, construction contracts must be in property location court

### Arbitration

#### Arbitration Law (2017 Amendment)
- **Arbitration Agreement**: Must be in writing, specify arbitration institution
- **Arbitrable Matters**: Contract disputes, property disputes
- **Non-Arbitrable Matters**: Administrative disputes, employment disputes, antitrust

#### China International Economic and Trade Arbitration Commission (CIETAC)
- **International Arbitration**: For contracts with foreign elements
- **Domestic Arbitration**: For domestic contracts
- **Arbitration Rules**: CIETAC Arbitration Rules (2021)
