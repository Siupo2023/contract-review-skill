## Universal Contract Review Checklist

**Applicable to:** All contract types

### Section 1: Basic Information
- [ ] Contract name matches actual content
- [ ] All party information is complete and accurate
- [ ] Signing date and location are specified
- [ ] Contract validity period is clear

### Section 2: Subject Matter
- [ ] Transaction subject is clearly defined
- [ ] Specifications, quantities, and standards are specified
- [ ] Delivery location and time are clear
- [ ] Acceptance criteria are defined

### Section 3: Payment Terms
- [ ] Payment amount is clear (figures and words match)
- [ ] Payment timing and conditions are specified
- [ ] Payment method is defined
- [ ] Tax arrangements are clear
- [ ] Invoice requirements are specified

### Section 4: Rights and Obligations
- [ ] Rights of both parties are clearly defined
- [ ] Obligations of both parties are clearly defined
- [ ] Rights and obligations are balanced
- [ ] Performance timeline is realistic

### Section 5: Risk Prevention
- [ ] Effectiveness conditions are clear
- [ ] Guarantee/security provisions are appropriate
- [ ] Termination conditions are specified
- [ ] Breach liability is clearly defined
- [ ] Force majeure clause is included
- [ ] Dispute resolution mechanism is specified

### Section 6: Contract Formalities
- [ ] Delivery clause is included
- [ ] Amendment clause is included
- [ ] Attachment list is complete
- [ ] Signing authority is verified

## Lease Agreement Checklist

**Applicable to:** Property lease agreements

### Additional Review Points

#### For Landlord (甲方)
- [ ] Rent amount and escalation clauses are favorable
- [ ] Security deposit amount is appropriate
- [ ] Use premises restrictions are specified
- [ ] Maintenance responsibility allocation is clear
- [ ] Right to enter premises for inspection is included
- [ ] Subletting restrictions are specified

#### For Tenant (乙方)
- [ ] Rent amount is market-competitive
- [ ] Security deposit return conditions are clear
- [ ] Use premises rights are sufficiently broad
- [ ] Maintenance obligations are reasonable
- [ ] Early termination rights are included
- [ ] Renewal options are specified

### Special Considerations
- [ ] Property description is detailed and accurate
- [ ] Utilities and service charges allocation is clear
- [ ] Parking rights (if applicable) are specified
- [ ] Renovation and alteration rights are defined
- [ ] Insurance requirements are specified
- [ ] Compliance with building regulations is addressed

## Construction Contract Checklist

**Applicable to:** Construction, engineering, and renovation contracts

### Additional Review Points

#### Pre-Construction
- [ ] Construction permits and approvals are addressed
- [ ] Site conditions are documented
- [ ] Design documents and drawings are referenced
- [ ] Survey and geotechnical reports are included

#### Construction Scope
- [ ] Scope of work is clearly defined
- [ ] Construction standards and specifications are detailed
- [ ] Materials and equipment specifications are provided
- [ ] Quality standards and acceptance criteria are defined

#### Timeline and Milestones
- [ ] Construction commencement date is fixed
- [ ] Completion milestones are defined
- [ ] Extension of time mechanisms are specified
- [ ] Liquidated damages for delay are specified

#### Payment Terms
- [ ] Payment milestones are linked to progress
- [ ] Retention money provisions are appropriate
- [ ] Final payment conditions are clear
- [ ] Variation order procedures are defined

#### Risk Allocation
- [ ] Price fluctuation risk is allocated
- [ ] Force majeure events are specified
- [ ] Underground conditions risk is allocated
- [ ] Third-party interference risk is addressed

#### Post-Construction
- [ ] Defects liability period is specified
- [ ] Warranty obligations are defined
- [ ] As-built documentation requirements are included
- [ ] Handover procedures are detailed

## Usage Instructions

### How to Use These Checklists

1. **Select Appropriate Checklist**: Choose the checklist that matches your contract type
2. **Systematic Review**: Go through each item sequentially
3. **Document Findings**: Mark issues and concerns for each item
4. **Prioritize Risks**: Focus on high-risk and mandatory items first
5. **Generate Report**: Include checklist findings in your review report

### Customization Guidelines

- Adapt checklists based on client-specific requirements
- Add industry-specific items as needed
- Modify risk thresholds based on client risk appetite
- Update regularly based on new regulations and case law
